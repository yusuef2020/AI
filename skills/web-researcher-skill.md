# In-Depth Web Researcher Skill for Claude

## Overview
This skill enhances Claude's capability to conduct comprehensive web research, analyze findings, compare resources, and provide valuable insights tailored to your specific requirements.

## Core Competencies

### 1. Research Strategy & Planning
- **Define Research Scope**: Clearly establish boundaries, target audience, and depth level required
- **Identify Key Questions**: Break down complex topics into specific research questions
- **Source Prioritization**: Rank sources by relevance, authority, recency, and credibility
- **Methodology Selection**: Choose appropriate research approaches (comparative, analytical, exploratory, evaluative)

### 2. Information Gathering & Analysis

#### Search & Discovery Phase
- Utilize multiple search angles and keyword variations
- Identify primary vs. secondary sources
- Evaluate source credibility and potential bias
- Cross-reference information across multiple sources
- Identify gaps in current information

#### Content Analysis Phase
- Extract key data points and statistics
- Identify patterns and trends
- Recognize contrasting viewpoints
- Distinguish facts from opinions
- Document evidence and citations

### 3. Comparison & Evaluation Framework

#### Systematic Comparison Matrix
When comparing resources, create analysis across these dimensions:
- **Relevance**: How applicable is this to your requirements?
- **Accuracy**: Is the information verifiable and current?
- **Depth**: Does it provide sufficient detail for your needs?
- **Authority**: Who is the source and what is their expertise?
- **Bias**: What potential biases might influence the content?
- **Completeness**: Does it address all key aspects of the topic?

#### Quality Assessment
- Identify peer-reviewed vs. opinion-based content
- Evaluate recency and whether information is outdated
- Check for citations and evidence backing claims
- Assess author credentials and organizational reputation
- Note any conflicts of interest

### 4. Insight Generation

#### Pattern Recognition
- Identify recurring themes across multiple sources
- Spot emerging trends and shifts in thinking
- Recognize consensus areas vs. areas of disagreement
- Find unexpected connections between data points

#### Critical Analysis
- Challenge assumptions and conventional wisdom
- Identify underlying causes and contributing factors
- Evaluate logical consistency of arguments
- Assess strengths and limitations of different perspectives

#### Synthesis & Synthesis
- Integrate findings into coherent narrative
- Highlight key takeaways and implications
- Identify areas needing further research
- Propose actionable recommendations based on findings

### 5. Reporting & Communication

#### Structure for Comprehensive Reports
1. **Executive Summary**: Key findings and main conclusions
2. **Research Methodology**: Approach taken and sources consulted
3. **Detailed Findings**: In-depth analysis organized by theme
4. **Comparative Analysis**: How resources and findings compare
5. **Key Insights**: What this means and why it matters
6. **Recommendations**: Action items or next steps
7. **Source Documentation**: Full citations and references

#### Presentation Guidelines
- Lead with most important findings
- Use data visualization where applicable
- Provide context for statistics and quotes
- Clearly distinguish between facts and interpretations
- Acknowledge limitations and uncertainties
- Include confidence levels for claims

## Research Methodologies

### 1. Exploratory Research
**When**: You need to understand a new or emerging topic
- Broad initial searches
- Identify key players and perspectives
- Map the landscape of the field
- Discover subtopics and related areas

### 2. Comparative Research
**When**: You need to evaluate options or compare approaches
- Identify comparable items/resources/approaches
- Create detailed comparison matrix
- Highlight advantages and disadvantages
- Provide recommendation framework

### 3. Evaluative Research
**When**: You need to assess quality, effectiveness, or value
- Establish evaluation criteria
- Measure against benchmarks
- Identify strengths and weaknesses
- Provide merit or quality assessment

### 4. Trend Analysis
**When**: You need to understand market movements or emerging patterns
- Identify historical data and patterns
- Spot inflection points and shifts
- Project future trajectories
- Explain underlying drivers of change

### 5. Expert Synthesis
**When**: You need opinions from thought leaders
- Find leading voices in the field
- Identify consensus vs. disagreement
- Present range of perspectives
- Synthesize into coherent overview

## Best Practices for Quality Research

### Source Evaluation Checklist
- [ ] Is the author/organization credible and established?
- [ ] Are claims backed by evidence and citations?
- [ ] Is the information current and regularly updated?
- [ ] Is there potential bias or conflict of interest?
- [ ] Does the source have a clear methodology?
- [ ] Are limitations and uncertainties acknowledged?
- [ ] Can findings be corroborated by other sources?

### Red Flags to Watch
- Extraordinary claims without evidence
- Heavy emotional language or inflammatory tone
- Absence of author credentials
- One-sided presentation of complex topics
- Outdated information presented as current
- Lack of supporting citations
- Sensationalist headlines not matching content

### Verification Techniques
- Fact-check through multiple independent sources
- Look for original research vs. secondary reporting
- Check publication dates and update frequency
- Cross-reference statistics and data points
- Verify author credentials and affiliations
- Review methodology for research claims
